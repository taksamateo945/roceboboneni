Reni Eddo-Lodge Bio & Family: Ethnicity, Wiki, Education

The British Journalist is 31-years of age, born on 25 September 1989 in London. She was raised by a Nigerian mother. At the age of four, she used to ask her mother when she would turn white. It was because of her false belief that all of the good people were white considering herself also to be good. 


Reni went to St Anne’s Catholic High School in Enfield and chose to study English literature at the University of Central Lancashire. Over there she did her graduation in 2011. 

Facts About Reni-Eddo-Lodge Award-Winning Book!

Reni’s debut book Why I’m No Longer Talking to White People About Race gained immense appreciation upon its release in mid-2017. The book is an exploration of the interconnectivity between gender, class, and race in the UK and other territories.


It begins with a summary of the experience of Asian and Black societies in the UK citing Indian Soldiers in WWI, Atlantic Slave Trade, Briston Bus Boycott, Labour Black Sections, and the 1981 Riots. Likewise, the book covers institutional racism in British society, white feminisms to define a class that only incorporates white people.
