Quick Facts

Lizzie Brochere childhood

Lizzie Brochere grew up in Paris, France, with her mother, Sylvie Brochere. She adopted her mother’s surname. Information about her father is unknown.

It is possible that her mother, Sylvie raised Lizzie singlehandedly. Growing up, she did not have much contact with her father. Lizzie’s mother, Sylvie Brochere, is a French casting director. Lizzie has not talked much about whether she has any siblings.

Because of her mother’s connections in the French acting world, Lizzie grew up keen on becoming an actress. She was able to build her network of acquaintances through her mother, and as a result, Lizzie made her debut at the age of 10. She made an appearance in the TV series titled Parents a mi-temps.

Lizzie Brochere career

Work in French TV

Lizzie stepped into the world of French TV when she debuted in 1995 with the show Parents a mi-temps. After this, Lizzie appeared in the show Trios en-un-Mission: Protection Raprochee in the year 1999. Lizzie was 14 years old when she worked on the show.

Lizzie became a household name when she increased her appearances in TV shows in the 2000s. Some of her notable works are Margaux Valence: Le Silenece d’Alice and Relic Hunter. In 2004, Lizzie had a recurring role as Eva in the TV series Alex Santana, negociateur.

Lizzie Brocheré French Movies

In 2002, Lizzie moved from TV shows to movies. Her debut movie was called Le Lou de La cote oust. Hugo Santiago directed the film. This movie was screened at the Montreal Film Festival and received mixed reviews.

Lizzie was also part of the controversial film Chacun Sa Nuit by Jean-Marc Barr and Pascal Arnold. In the movie, Lizzie played the role of Lucie. The film generated many controversies as it was based on incest and included a lot of nudity.

Lizzie reunited with Jean-Marc Barr and Pascal Arnold when she signed the critically acclaimed movie titled American Translation. It was a sexy psychological thriller movie. Her first action movie was Nuit Blance, directed by Frederic Jardin.

Lizzie Brochere Appearance in Music Videos

Lizzie has also appeared in a music video called Song Flowers: The Micronauts Remix by Magic Pop Hotel, released in 2010.

English Speaking Roles and Work in Hollywood

After the 2010s, Lizzie transitioned from French to more English-speaking roles. Her first film where she fully spoke in English was After Fall, Winter. This dark adult comedic drama was released in 2011.

Due to her fantastic career graph and critically acclaimed performances, Lizzie Brochere was able to enter Hollywood.

She bagged a role in Brad Falchuk and Ryan Murphy’s show American Horror Story: Asylum, where Lizzie played the role of Grace Bertrand in the show’s second season. She was a part of the series for a year, from 2012 to 2013.

Lizzie played the titular role in Rings, a supernatural horror film made in the US. She also played the role of Coco in Guillermo del Toro’s The Strain.

Her international TV credits include the French TV series Versailles, BBC’s The Hour, and Canal+’s Braquo. Also, she played the lead role in Gale Anne Hurd’s sci-fi show Falling Water which airs on the USA and Amazon.

Brochere’s work in Hollywood helped her grab the American audiences’ attention, which allowed her to establish herself as a talented actress to watch out for.

Lizzie Brocheré as Director, Writer, and Producer

In addition to being a prominent actress in French and American showbiz, Lizzie Brochere has also exhibited her talent as a director, writer, and producer.

She wrote, produced, and directed the French short film called Terre ferme, released in 2017.

Lizzie also produced the short environmental documentary Une Zone Humide à Défendre through her non-profit organization Lemanja. The film was written and directed by Lizzie while closely working with refugees to explore everyday racism.

Lizzie Brocheré awards and recognitions

For her fantastic performance in the controversial film Chacun Sa Nuit by Jean-Marc Barr and Pascal Arnold, Lizzie was pre-selected in Best Newcomer in the French Cesars. She also won the award for Best Newcomer at Luchon Film Festival for her outstanding performance in Bac+70.

Lizzie was also honored with the Best Actress award due to the complex role of Myriam that she depicted in Le Chant des Mariees. The award was presented to her during St. de Luz Film Festival.

Her performance in the film Full Contact helped her win the Best Female Actress Award at the Chicago International Film Festival.

Social Activism

Lizzie Brochere is one of the co-founders of the non-profit organization called Iemanja. The organization aims to promote sustainable environmental awareness and create dialogues between cultures and generations. Iemanja also works to apply tools of performance to social integration techniques.
